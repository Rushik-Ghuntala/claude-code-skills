# Article Templates

Fill-in-the-blank templates for each article type. Replace all [PLACEHOLDER] text with your specific content. Word count targets are listed per section to help with pacing.

## Template 1: Technical Tutorial

**Target total length**: 900-1500 words

---

### [Title: Number + Topic + Outcome]

[HOOK: State the specific problem your reader faces and the concrete outcome they will achieve by the end of this tutorial. Include the primary keyword naturally. Mention a specific metric or result if possible.]

*Target: 50-100 words*

### Why [TOPIC] Matters Right Now

[CONTEXT: Explain why this technique, tool, or pattern is relevant today. Reference a specific trend, pain point, or recent change that makes this timely. State who benefits most from learning this — what role, what scale of project, what stage of development.]

[STAKES: What happens if the reader ignores this? Not fear-mongering, but a concrete consequence — slower builds, security vulnerabilities, lost developer time, poor user experience.]

*Target: 100-150 words*

### Prerequisites

Before starting, make sure you have:

- [TOOL/LANGUAGE] version [VERSION] or higher ([link to install])
- [DEPENDENCY] installed globally or in your project
- Basic familiarity with [PRIOR KNOWLEDGE CONCEPT]
- [OPTIONAL: Access to SERVICE or API key for SERVICE]

*Target: 50-75 words*

### Step 1: [ACTION VERB] [OBJECT]

[EXPLANATION: In 1-2 sentences, explain what this step accomplishes and why it comes first.]

```[language]
[COMPLETE, RUNNABLE CODE with imports and realistic variable names]
```

[RESULT: What the reader should see or have after this step. Expected output, created files, or state change.]

*Target: 75-150 words per step*

### Step 2: [ACTION VERB] [OBJECT]

[EXPLANATION: What this step builds on from Step 1 and what new capability it adds.]

```[language]
[CODE BLOCK — building on Step 1, with any new imports shown]
```

[RESULT: Expected state after this step.]

### Step 3: [ACTION VERB] [OBJECT]

[EXPLANATION: The core implementation step. This is usually where the main logic lives.]

```[language]
[CODE BLOCK — the meat of the tutorial]
```

[RESULT: The reader should now have a working version of the core feature.]

### Step 4: [ACTION VERB] [OBJECT]

[EXPLANATION: Polish, testing, or extension step. Adding error handling, tests, or a finishing touch.]

```[language]
[CODE BLOCK — hardening or completing the implementation]
```

[RESULT: The final working state.]

### Common Pitfalls

- **[PITFALL NAME]**: [Symptom the reader might see]. This happens because [root cause]. Fix it by [specific action].
- **[PITFALL NAME]**: [Symptom]. Caused by [root cause]. The fix is [specific action].
- **[PITFALL NAME]**: [Symptom]. Usually because [root cause]. [Specific action] resolves it.

*Target: 100-200 words*

### Key Takeaways

1. [ACTIONABLE LESSON starting with a verb — "Use X when Y"]
2. [ACTIONABLE LESSON]
3. [ACTIONABLE LESSON]
4. [OPTIONAL FOURTH LESSON]

*Target: 75-100 words*

### What's Next

[CTA: Suggest an extension of the project, a related topic to explore, or invite the reader to share their results. Include a specific next step, not a generic "keep learning."]

*Target: 50-75 words*

## Template 2: Deep Dive / Explainer

**Target total length**: 1000-1800 words

---

### [Title: "How X Actually Works" or "X Under the Hood" or "The Truth About X"]

[HOOK: Open with a surprising fact, a widespread misconception, or a provocative question. Make the reader think "wait, really?" within the first two sentences.]

*Target: 50-100 words*

### Background

[CONTEXT: What does the reader need to know to follow this deep dive? Cover only directly relevant history, ecosystem context, or prerequisite concepts. Define any non-obvious terms. State what the article will and will not cover.]

*Target: 150-200 words*

### [SIMPLIFIED MENTAL MODEL HEADING]

[LAYER 1: Describe what most developers think happens. Use a clear analogy. This is the "good enough for everyday use" understanding.]

*Target: 100-150 words*

### [WHAT REALLY HAPPENS HEADING]

[LAYER 2: Peel back the abstraction. Show what actually occurs step by step. Use a code example or diagram that demonstrates the real behavior.]

```[language or ascii]
[CODE EXAMPLE or ASCII DIAGRAM showing the actual mechanism]
```

[EXPLANATION: Walk through the code or diagram, explaining each part and how it differs from the simplified model.]

*Target: 200-300 words*

### [EDGE CASES / NUANCE HEADING]

[LAYER 3: Where the simple explanation breaks down. Cover edge cases, performance implications, or historical reasons for surprising behavior. This is the "aha" section.]

```[language]
[CODE EXAMPLE demonstrating an edge case or surprising behavior]
```

[EXPLANATION: Why this happens and what it means.]

*Target: 150-250 words*

### What This Means in Practice

[IMPLICATIONS: Now that the reader understands the mechanism, how should it change their decisions? What assumptions should they revisit? What common mistakes does this understanding help them avoid?]

*Target: 100-200 words*

### What to Do With This Knowledge

1. [SPECIFIC ACTION the reader can take]
2. [SPECIFIC ACTION]
3. [OPTIONAL THIRD ACTION]

*Target: 100-150 words*

### Further Exploration

[CTA: Point to source code, documentation, related topics, or an experiment the reader can try to deepen their understanding.]

*Target: 50-75 words*

## Template 3: Opinion / Hot Take (Long-form)

**Target total length**: 800-1400 words

---

### [Title: "Why X Is Y" or "Stop Doing X" or "X Is the Wrong Default"]

[THESIS: State your position directly. No warm-up, no hedge. Be specific enough that someone could disagree. Include who this opinion applies to and what context you are speaking from.]

*Target: 75-100 words*

### [DECLARATIVE STATEMENT — ARGUMENT 1]

[EVIDENCE: Your strongest argument. Use a concrete example with specific details — a company, a project, a codebase, a metric. Not "in my experience, things tend to..." but "at [Company], we saw [specific outcome] after [specific action]."]

[ANALYSIS: Why this example supports your thesis. Connect the specific to the general.]

*Target: 150-250 words*

### [DECLARATIVE STATEMENT — ARGUMENT 2]

[EVIDENCE: A different type of evidence. If Argument 1 was personal experience, this should be industry data, a technical analysis, or a well-known case study. Variety in evidence types strengthens the overall argument.]

[ANALYSIS: How this evidence reinforces the thesis from a different angle.]

*Target: 150-250 words*

### [DECLARATIVE STATEMENT — ARGUMENT 3]

[EVIDENCE: A forward-looking or principled argument. This can be more speculative — you have earned the reader's trust with the first two concrete arguments.]

[ANALYSIS: Connect this to the broader trajectory of the technology or practice.]

*Target: 150-200 words*

### The Counterarguments

[OBJECTION 1: State the best counterargument in its strongest form. Respond honestly. Concede where appropriate.]

[OBJECTION 2: Another strong counterargument. Respond with nuance, not dismissal.]

[SYNTHESIS: Explain when your position does and does not apply. This nuance strengthens rather than weakens your argument.]

*Target: 150-200 words*

### The Bottom Line

[CONCLUSION: Restate the thesis in light of the evidence. Do not simply repeat the introduction. Add synthesis — what should change? What should the reader reconsider?]

*Target: 75-100 words*

### Join the Conversation

[CTA: Invite genuine discussion. Ask a specific question. Frame it as a conversation, not a debate.]

*Target: 50-75 words*

---

*Target: 50-100 words*

**Why it stands out**: [1-2 sentences on specific strengths, not generic praise]

**Best for**: [The specific use case or scenario where this item shines]

```[language]

```

**Watch out for**: [One honest limitation or caveat]

*Target: 75-150 words per item*

### 2. [ITEM NAME] — [One-line Description]

**Why it stands out**: [Specific strengths]

**Best for**: [Specific use case]

```[language]
[CODE EXAMPLE or KEY LINK]
```

**Watch out for**: [Limitation]

### 3. [ITEM NAME] — [One-line Description]

**Why it stands out**: [Specific strengths]

**Best for**: [Specific use case]

```[language]
[CODE EXAMPLE or KEY LINK]
```

**Watch out for**: [Limitation]

[REPEAT FOR REMAINING ITEMS — aim for 5-10 items total]

### Where to Start

[SUMMARY: What patterns emerge across the items? If the reader can only try one thing today, which item should it be and why?]

*Target: 75-100 words*

### What Did I Miss?

[CTA: Invite the reader to suggest additions, share their favorites, or report their experience with the listed items.]

*Target: 50-75 words*

## Template 5: Case Study / Postmortem

**Target total length**: 1000-1800 words

---

### [Title: "How We [OUTCOME] by [ACTION]" or "[TOPIC]: A Postmortem" or "What Happened When We [ACTION]"]

### The Setup

[CONTEXT: Describe the system, team, and situation before the story begins. Include specific metrics — user count, request volume, team size, codebase age, technology stack. Help the reader gauge whether your experience is relevant to their context.]

*Target: 100-150 words*

### The Problem

[SYMPTOMS: What went wrong? Be specific — error messages, metric changes, user reports. Include the moment of realization.]

[IMPACT: Quantify the cost. Downtime, lost revenue, user churn, developer hours spent, missed deadlines.]

*Target: 100-200 words*

### What We Tried

**Approach 1: [NAME]**
[HYPOTHESIS: What we thought the problem was]
[ACTION: What we did to test the hypothesis]
[RESULT: Why it did not work]

**Approach 2: [NAME]**
[HYPOTHESIS]
[ACTION]
[RESULT]

**Approach 3: [NAME]**
[HYPOTHESIS]
[ACTION]
[RESULT — this one might be the one that partially worked or led to the real solution]

*Target: 200-300 words*

### What Worked

[SOLUTION: Describe the approach that succeeded. Include implementation details.]

```[language]
[KEY CODE CHANGE or CONFIGURATION CHANGE]
```

[WHY IT WORKED: Explain what was different about this approach. Why did it succeed where the others failed?]

*Target: 200-300 words*

### Results

| Metric | Before | After | Improvement |
|--------|--------|-------|-------------|
| [METRIC 1] | [VALUE] | [VALUE] | [PERCENTAGE or ABSOLUTE CHANGE] |
| [METRIC 2] | [VALUE] | [VALUE] | [CHANGE] |
| [METRIC 3] | [VALUE] | [VALUE] | [CHANGE] |

[TIMELINE: When did results materialize? Immediately? Over days or weeks?]

*Target: 100-150 words*

### Lessons Learned

1. **[PRINCIPLE]**: [One sentence explanation of how this applies beyond your specific case]
2. **[PRINCIPLE]**: [Explanation]
3. **[PRINCIPLE]**: [Explanation]
4. **[OPTIONAL PRINCIPLE]**: [Explanation]
5. **[OPTIONAL PRINCIPLE]**: [Explanation]

*Target: 100-200 words*

### Questions Welcome

[CTA: Invite readers to ask about specific aspects of the case study or share their own similar experiences.]

*Target: 50-75 words*

## Template 6: Career / Developer Insights

**Target total length**: 800-1200 words

---

### [Title: "The [LESSON] Nobody Told Me About [TOPIC]" or "What [EXPERIENCE] Taught Me About [INSIGHT]"]

[STORY HOOK: Drop the reader into a specific moment. Not "I've been in tech for 10 years" but a vivid scene — a meeting, a late night, a conversation, a failure. Include sensory or contextual details that make the moment real.]

*Target: 100-150 words*

### [THE INSIGHT AS A HEADING — stated as a principle, not "The Lesson"]

[INSIGHT: State the core lesson clearly. Explain why it is non-obvious or underappreciated. Identify the common assumption it challenges. This is the thesis of the article.]

[CONTEXT: How did you arrive at this insight? What shifted in your thinking?]

*Target: 150-200 words*

### [EXAMPLE 1 HEADING]

[EXAMPLE: A different situation where the same lesson applied. Different company, role, or technology. Show the pattern, not just the single instance.]

*Target: 75-100 words*

### [EXAMPLE 2 HEADING]

[EXAMPLE: Another context, same underlying pattern. This example should add a new dimension — maybe the lesson applied in a surprising domain or at a different career stage.]

*Target: 75-100 words*

### [EXAMPLE 3 HEADING — optional]

[EXAMPLE: A third instance, ideally from someone else's experience or a publicly known story. This broadens the evidence beyond your personal career.]

*Target: 75-100 words*

### What You Can Do About It

1. **[SPECIFIC ACTION]**: [What to do, when to do it, and how. Specific enough that the reader could start today.]
2. **[SPECIFIC ACTION]**: [Details]
3. **[SPECIFIC ACTION]**: [Details]
4. **[OPTIONAL ACTION]**: [Details]
5. **[OPTIONAL ACTION]**: [Details]

*Target: 150-200 words*

### Over to You

[CTA: Ask the reader a specific question about their own experience with this topic. Invite reflection, not just agreement. Career content benefits from a sense of community and shared experience.]

*Target: 50-75 words*
