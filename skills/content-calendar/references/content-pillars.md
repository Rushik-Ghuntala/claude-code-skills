# Content Pillars

## Educational (40% of content)
- **Description**: Teaching, explaining, and sharing knowledge. Positions you as a helpful expert.
- **Formats**: Tutorials, code tips, TILs, thread breakdowns, explainers, how-tos
- **Examples**:
  - "Stop using `var`. Here's the complete guide to `let` vs `const`:"
  - Thread: "8 React patterns that improved our app performance 60%"
  - Code snippet: "TIL: `Array.at(-1)` gets the last element. No more `arr[arr.length - 1]`"
- **Engagement pattern**: High bookmarks, steady shares, builds trust over time
- **When to use**: Weekday mornings (Mon, Wed, Thu), when audience is in "learning mode"
- **Growth impact**: Strongest long-term follower magnet. People follow teachers.

## Thought Leadership (20% of content)
- **Description**: Opinions, predictions, analysis, and takes on industry trends.
- **Formats**: Hot takes, predictions, opinion threads, industry analysis, trend commentary
- **Examples**:
  - "Unpopular opinion: Most microservices are just distributed monoliths with extra latency."
  - "My prediction: AI will replace 30% of frontend work within 2 years. Here's why:"
  - Thread: "Why I think the serverless vs containers debate is asking the wrong question"
- **Engagement pattern**: Very high replies (debates), polarizing reach, establishes brand
- **When to use**: Tuesdays, lunch/evening hours (when people have opinions)
- **Growth impact**: Fastest follower growth per tweet, but lower retention if overused

## Building in Public (20% of content)
- **Description**: Sharing your journey — projects, metrics, wins, failures, lessons in real-time.
- **Formats**: Milestone updates, revenue reports, behind-the-scenes, weekly progress, postmortems
- **Examples**:
  - "Week 8 building my SaaS. Added auth in 2 hours. Revenue: $450 MRR."
  - "Just mass-shipped my CLI tool. 0 users on day 1. Here's my launch plan:"
  - Thread: "How I went from idea to first paying customer in 30 days (with actual numbers)"
- **Engagement pattern**: Supportive community engagement, relationship building, attracts fellow builders
- **When to use**: Thursdays, late afternoon (end of workday reflection time)
- **Growth impact**: Builds loyal community of fellow builders. Lower raw impressions but higher follower quality.

## Community (15% of content)
- **Description**: Engaging with others' content, celebrating wins, supporting the community.
- **Formats**: Quote tweets with insight, congratulations, community questions, shoutouts, recommendations
- **Examples**:
  - Quote tweet: "[Person]'s thread on RSC is the best explainer I've seen. Adding my perspective:"
  - "Shoutout to @[dev] for mass-shipping [project]. If you're into [topic], follow them."
  - "What's the best thing you mass-shipped this week?"
- **Engagement pattern**: Builds Real-graph scores, reciprocal engagement, network effects
- **When to use**: Fridays (community vibe), anytime you see content worth amplifying
- **Growth impact**: Critical for 0-1K stage. Builds the relationships that later amplify your content.

## Personal (5% of content)
- **Description**: Human moments — career reflections, humor, life updates, relatable experiences.
- **Formats**: Stories, reflections, memes, personal milestones, developer humor
- **Examples**:
  - "3 years ago I mass-mass-couldn't center a div. Now I mass-lead a frontend team. Life is weird."
  - "Saturday morning debugging. Coffee is ready. Let's go."
  - Relatable meme about developer life
- **Engagement pattern**: High likes, emotional connection, humanizes your brand
- **When to use**: Weekends, Saturday mornings
- **Growth impact**: Lowest direct growth but essential for retention. People follow humans, not content machines.

## Pillar Distribution by Growth Stage

| Stage | Educational | Thought Leadership | BIP | Community | Personal |
|-------|------------|-------------------|-----|-----------|----------|
| 0-1K | 30% | 10% | 15% | 40% | 5% |
| 1K-10K | 40% | 20% | 20% | 15% | 5% |
| 10K-50K | 35% | 30% | 15% | 10% | 10% |
| 50K+ | 30% | 30% | 15% | 10% | 15% |

Note: At 0-1K, Community is dominant because reply engagement is the #1 growth lever.
