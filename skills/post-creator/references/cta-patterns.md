# Call-to-Action Patterns

## Soft CTAs (Low friction, high compliance)
- "Bookmark this." — triggers bookmark signal (40x weight)
- "Save this for later." — variant of bookmark CTA
- "What do you think?" — open-ended reply driver
- "Share if this helped." — retweet nudge without being pushy
- "RT if you agree." — only use when genuinely opinion-based, avoid for factual content

## Hard CTAs (Higher friction, direct conversion)
- "Follow for more [niche] content." — only in high-value posts (don't overuse)
- "Check out [project] (link in reply)." — redirect to self-reply for link
- "Subscribe to my newsletter for the deep dive." — funnel to owned media
- "Star the repo: [name] (link in reply)." — open source promotion

## Engagement CTAs (Designed to drive algorithm-boosting replies)
- "What's your experience with this?" — invites personal stories
- "Agree or disagree?" — creates binary debate
- "What would you add to this list?" — crowdsources and drives replies
- "Drop your favorite [tool/tip/hack] below." — gamifies participation
- "Am I wrong?" — contrarian reply driver
- "What am I missing?" — invites correction (devs love correcting)

## Dev-Specific CTAs
- "Star the repo." — GitHub promotion
- "Try this in your terminal right now." — hands-on engagement
- "What's your stack?" — identity question that devs love answering
- "Benchmark this on your machine and share the results." — interactive challenge
- "What version are you on?" — drives community comparison
- "Ship it or skip it?" — binary choice on a tool/framework

## Format-Specific CTA Recommendations
| Format | Best CTA Type | Example |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| Value Bomb | Soft (bookmark) | "Bookmark this. You'll need it." |
| Hot Take | Engagement (debate) | "Agree or disagree?" |
| Story | Engagement (share) | "What's your version of this story?" |
| Question | Engagement (reply) | "Drop yours below." |
| Code Snippet | Soft (bookmark) | "Save for your next project." |
| Announcement | Hard (follow) | "Follow along for updates." |
| Meme | Soft (share) | "Tag a dev who does this." |

## Anti-Patterns (Avoid)
- "Like if you agree" — flagged as engagement bait by algorithm
- "Follow for follow" — reduces account quality signals
- "Please retweet" — desperate energy, penalized
- Multiple CTAs in one tweet — confusing, reduces action rate
- CTA that doesn't match content type — feels inauthentic
