# 15 Proven Hook Archetypes for Developer Content

## 1. Contrarian
- **Template**: "Unpopular opinion: [controversial take about widely-accepted practice]"
- **When to use**: When you have a genuine alternative perspective backed by experience
- **Example**: "Unpopular opinion: TypeScript slows down prototyping more than it prevents bugs."
- **Why it works**: Triggers agree/disagree responses (reply weight = 54x). People can't resist defending their position.
- **Ideal length**: 80-140 characters
- **Best voice**: Contrarian, Data Nerd

## 2. Curiosity Gap
- **Template**: "I [did specific thing]. [Surprising result]. Here's what happened:"
- **When to use**: When you have a real experience with unexpected results
- **Example**: "I mass-migrated 47 components to RSC. 40% less JavaScript shipped. Here's the ugly truth nobody's sharing:"
- **Why it works**: Specific numbers create credibility; "ugly truth" implies hidden knowledge
- **Ideal length**: 140-200 characters
- **Best voice**: Builder, Data Nerd

## 3. Social Proof + Surprise
- **Template**: "After [impressive credential/action], [unexpected insight]"
- **When to use**: When you have authority to share and a non-obvious conclusion
- **Example**: "After mass-interviewing 50 senior devs, I found that none of them could explain useEffect cleanup correctly."
- **Why it works**: Authority + surprise creates must-read tension
- **Ideal length**: 120-180 characters
- **Best voice**: Teacher, Data Nerd

## 4. Pain Point
- **Template**: "The #1 mistake developers make with [technology]:"
- **When to use**: When you've identified a common error that has a clear fix
- **Example**: "The #1 mistake developers make with React Server Components (I made it for 3 months):"
- **Why it works**: Nobody wants to be making THE biggest mistake. Parenthetical adds relatability.
- **Ideal length**: 80-140 characters
- **Best voice**: Teacher, Storyteller

## 5. Data/Metric Lead
- **Template**: "I benchmarked [X vs Y]. [Surprising metric]. [Teaser]:"
- **When to use**: When you have actual numbers or performance comparisons
- **Example**: "I benchmarked RSC vs CSR on a real production app. The results surprised me."
- **Why it works**: Developers love data. "Surprised me" implies the results challenge assumptions.
- **Ideal length**: 100-160 characters
- **Best voice**: Data Nerd, Builder

## 6. Story Opener
- **Template**: "[Time reference] I [relatable situation]. [Current contrast]."
- **When to use**: For career stories, personal growth, project journeys
- **Example**: "2 years ago I mass-applied to 200 jobs. 3 interviews. Now companies DM me."
- **Why it works**: Before/after contrast creates narrative tension. Readers want the "how."
- **Ideal length**: 100-160 characters
- **Best voice**: Storyteller, Builder

## 7. Direct Value
- **Template**: "Stop [common practice]. [Better alternative]. [Why]."
- **When to use**: When you have a clearly better approach to share
- **Example**: "Stop using console.log for debugging. Use console.table() instead. It formats arrays and objects as a table. Try it."
- **Why it works**: "Stop" is a pattern interrupt. Immediate actionable value = bookmarks (40x signal).
- **Ideal length**: 140-220 characters
- **Best voice**: Teacher, Contrarian

## 8. Question Hook
- **Template**: "What's the one [thing] you'd [action] if [constraint]?"
- **When to use**: To drive replies and start conversations
- **Example**: "What's the one tool you'd remove from your stack if forced? Mine: Docker Compose"
- **Why it works**: Questions drive replies (54x). Adding your own answer models the response format.
- **Ideal length**: 80-140 characters
- **Best voice**: Any voice

## 9. List Promise
- **Template**: "[Number] things I wish I knew about [topic] [time ago]:"
- **When to use**: For listicle threads or experience-based advice
- **Example**: "7 things I wish I knew about PostgreSQL before using it in production:"
- **Why it works**: Numbered lists set clear expectations. "Wish I knew" implies hard-won wisdom.
- **Ideal length**: 80-120 characters
- **Best voice**: Teacher, Storyteller

## 10. Before/After
- **Template**: "I [action]. Before: [bad metric]. After: [good metric]."
- **When to use**: When you have concrete improvement metrics
- **Example**: "I rewrote our app with App Router. Before: 3s load time. After: 200ms."
- **Why it works**: Concrete metrics are undeniable. The gap creates "how did they do it?" curiosity.
- **Ideal length**: 100-160 characters
- **Best voice**: Builder, Data Nerd

## 11. Myth Buster
- **Template**: "Everyone says [common belief]. They're wrong. [Counter-evidence]."
- **When to use**: When you can genuinely disprove a widely-held belief
- **Example**: "Everyone says you need to learn DSA to get hired at FAANG. I got hired at Google without a single LeetCode problem."
- **Why it works**: Challenges tribal knowledge. Creates debate (replies) and shares (social currency).
- **Ideal length**: 120-200 characters
- **Best voice**: Contrarian, Storyteller

## 12. Behind the Scenes
- **Template**: "Here's what actually happens when you [common action]:"
- **When to use**: For explainer content about internals/hidden processes
- **Example**: "Here's what actually happens when you run npm install (it's more complex than you think):"
- **Why it works**: "Actually" implies hidden knowledge. Developers love understanding internals.
- **Ideal length**: 80-140 characters
- **Best voice**: Teacher, Data Nerd

## 13. Challenge
- **Template**: "Try this: [specific action]. [Expected result]. [Implied insight]."
- **When to use**: For interactive engagement and teaching moments
- **Example**: "Try this: delete your node_modules and reinstall. Check the size before and after. You'll be horrified."
- **Why it works**: Interactive hooks drive engagement. People try it and reply with their results.
- **Ideal length**: 120-180 characters
- **Best voice**: Teacher, Meme Lord

## 14. Time-Bound Urgency
- **Template**: "[Company/project] just [action]. Here's why it matters for [audience]:"
- **When to use**: For news/release commentary (must post within 6 hours of event)
- **Example**: "React just mass-shipped React 19 stable. Here's why it changes everything for Next.js developers:"
- **Why it works**: Recency boosts algorithmic ranking. First-mover advantage on commentary.
- **Ideal length**: 100-160 characters
- **Best voice**: Any voice

## 15. Identity Hook
- **Template**: "If you're a [role] and you don't know [thing], read this:"
- **When to use**: For targeting specific developer segments with must-know info
- **Example**: "If you're a senior dev and you don't know about structured clone, read this:"
- **Why it works**: Identity targeting makes it personal. "Don't know" creates fear of missing out.
- **Ideal length**: 80-140 characters
- **Best voice**: Teacher, Contrarian
