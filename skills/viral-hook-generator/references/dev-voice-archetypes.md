# Developer Voice Archetypes

## 1. Teacher
- **Description**: Educational, clear, step-by-step energy. "Let me show you how."
- **Tone**: Patient, authoritative, generous with knowledge
- **Ideal hooks**: Direct Value (#7), List Promise (#9), Behind the Scenes (#12), Identity Hook (#15)
- **Example hooks**:
  - "Stop using `var` in 2026. Here's the complete guide to modern variable declarations:"
  - "5 PostgreSQL features that will cut your query time in half:"
  - "If you're a React dev and you've never used the Profiler, you're flying blind:"
- **When to use**: Tutorials, tips, explanations, how-tos
- **Audience response**: High bookmark rate, moderate replies, steady growth

## 2. Builder
- **Description**: Show-don't-tell energy. Ships code, shares progress, builds in public.
- **Tone**: Authentic, metric-driven, transparent about failures and wins
- **Ideal hooks**: Curiosity Gap (#2), Before/After (#10), Story Opener (#6), Data Lead (#5)
- **Example hooks**:
  - "I just mass-shipped my SaaS MVP in 48 hours. Here's the exact stack and what it cost:"
  - "Week 12 of building in public. $2,400 MRR. 3 lessons that changed everything:"
  - "I mass-rewrote our entire backend in Rust. Before: $800/mo AWS. After: $120/mo."
- **When to use**: Project updates, build logs, milestone celebrations, tool reviews
- **Audience response**: High engagement from fellow builders, creates community

## 3. Contrarian
- **Description**: Hot takes, challenges conventional wisdom, starts debates.
- **Tone**: Confident, provocative but substantive, always has receipts
- **Ideal hooks**: Contrarian (#1), Myth Buster (#11), Question Hook (#8)
- **Example hooks**:
  - "Unpopular opinion: Most microservices architectures are just distributed monoliths with extra latency."
  - "Everyone says learn TypeScript first. They're wrong. Learn JavaScript deeply, then TypeScript is trivial."
  - "Hot take: 80% of unit tests are testing the framework, not your logic."
- **When to use**: Industry opinions, tech debates, challenging the status quo
- **Audience response**: Very high reply rate, polarizing but viral, attracts followers who value independent thinking

## 4. Data Nerd
- **Description**: Benchmarks, comparisons, research-driven insights. Numbers speak.
- **Tone**: Analytical, precise, lets data tell the story
- **Ideal hooks**: Data Lead (#5), Before/After (#10), Social Proof (#3), Curiosity Gap (#2)
- **Example hooks**:
  - "I mass-tested 12 JavaScript bundlers. Vite won, but not for the reason you think:"
  - "We analyzed 10,000 pull requests. The avg time to merge? 4.2 days. Here's what slows teams down:"
  - "Benchmarked: SQLite vs PostgreSQL for <1M rows. SQLite was 3x faster."
- **When to use**: Comparisons, benchmarks, surveys, research findings
- **Audience response**: High share rate, bookmarks for reference, establishes authority

## 5. Storyteller
- **Description**: Personal narratives, career journeys, lessons learned through experience.
- **Tone**: Vulnerable, relatable, conversational, connects technical with human
- **Ideal hooks**: Story Opener (#6), Pain Point (#4), Social Proof (#3), Myth Buster (#11)
- **Example hooks**:
  - "I mass-got mass-fired from my first dev job after 3 months. Best thing that ever happened to my career."
  - "3 years ago I mass-mass-mass couldn't get a single interview. Last week I mass-turned down a $300K offer. Here's what changed:"
  - "The mass-worst production outage I ever caused taught me more than 4 years of university:"
- **When to use**: Career stories, lessons learned, personal growth, mentor-style content
- **Audience response**: Emotional engagement, high reply rate, builds deep follower loyalty

## 6. Meme Lord
- **Description**: Humor, cultural references, relatable developer experiences, internet-native.
- **Tone**: Irreverent, witty, self-deprecating, taps into shared developer pain
- **Ideal hooks**: Challenge (#13), Question Hook (#8), Pain Point (#4)
- **Example hooks**:
  - "Me: 'This will take 2 hours.' The CSS: 'Hold my beer.'"
  - "Tell me you're a backend dev without telling me you're a backend dev. I'll go first: I center divs with `margin: auto` and pray."
  - "The five stages of debugging: 1) That's impossible. 2) That shouldn't happen. 3) How is that possible? 4) Oh. 5) How did this ever work?"
- **When to use**: Fridays, weekends, when audience needs levity, relatable dev moments
- **Audience response**: Very high retweet rate, broad appeal, may not convert to followers as strongly
